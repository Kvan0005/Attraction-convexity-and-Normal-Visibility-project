Glossary

Edges And Trajectories

  • Attraction Trajectory [AT(p,b)]: Indicate the movement path of a point p from its original location to the beacon b
  • Pocket Lid: an edge which is part of the convex hull of a polygon but not part of the polygon itself
  • Pocket Chain: A sequence of edges from the polygon which connect the two vertex of a pocket lid
  • Pull Edge: Straight movement towards the beacon
  • Separation Edge: A split edge between a dead region and AR(b)
  • Slide Edges: Consecutive sliding movements
  • Split Edge: The boundary between two dead regions, or a dead region and AR(b)

Edges set

  • Chain: A series of edges.
  • Monotone chain with respect to a line \(L\): A Monotone Chain with respect to \(L\) is a chain \(C\) for which every line orthogonal to \(L\) intersect \(C\) at most once.

Points

  • Dead Point: A point that remains stationary in the attraction pull of a beacon b

Polygons

  • Attraction-Convex Polygon: A polygon where every point attracts every other point
  • Convex Hull: The convex hull of a polygon is the smallest convex polygon that contains all the points of the polygon
  • Monotone Polygon with respect to a line \(L\): A Monotone Polygon with respect to \(L\) is a Polygon \(P\) for which every line orthogonal to \(L\) intersects the boundary of \(P\) at most twice.
  • Normal Visible Polygon: A polygon which the normal vector of each edge that is pointing towards the exterior cannot hit any point of the interior of the polygon
  • Pocket polygon: the polygon formed by the pocket chain and the pocket lid
  • Simple Polygon: A Simple Polygon is a polygon that does not intersect itself and has no holes

Regions

  • Attraction-Convex Region: A region where every point attracts every other point
  • Attraction Region [AR]: The region of points that are attracted to a beacon
  • Dead Region: The set of all points in a polygon that eventually reach (and stay) on a dead point
  • Inverse Attraction Region [IAR]: The region of points that attract a beacon

Shortest Path

  • Shortest Path [SP (p,q)]: The shortest path between p and q in P
  • Shortest Path Map [SPM (p)]: A shortest path map for a given point p, denoted as SPM (p), is a subdivision of P into regions such that shortest paths from p to all the points inside the same region pass through the same set of vertices of P
  • Shortest Path Tree [SPT (p)]: The union of shortest paths from p to all vertices of P

Vertex

  • Convex Vertex: A convex vertex \(v\) of a polygon \(P\) is a vertex where the internal angle with the two edges of \(v\) is lower than 180 degrees
  • Reflex Vertex: A reflex vertex \(v\) of a polygon \(P\) is a vertex where the internal angle with the two edges of \(v\) exceeds 180 degrees
